Co-curricullar activities are very important both for the well growth and relaxation for refreshment after a day of hardwork in the classes. The students really need to be exposed to Clubs that allow them to grow in a good environment. Kagumo High school has clubs such as:

  1. Wildlife Club
  2. Music Club
  3. Debate Club
  4. St. John's Ambulance Club
  5. Kenya Scouts Club
  6. Anti-drugs Club
  7. Art Club
  8. Coding Club

The clubs have teachers as their patrons who ensure that they run their day to day business smoothly. The clubs leadership is exemplorary therefore gaining the confidence of both the Administration and the members. One of the club leaders Jeremiah Kiptis, Coding Club said Coding is the language of the feuture and Kagumo High School, via the Coding Club, has given us the chance to learn it.

The music club, together with their patron Mr. Kamithi, have been appearing in the National Music Festivals and performing very well. This has made the principal, Dr. Mwirigi, very proud of the school.

The Board of Trustees, Parents Association and the entire fraternity is pleased with the work the clubs are doing to enhance students' knowledge, the environment they are offering to them other than class work together with the confidence gained by the students via, for example, the Debate Club whose previous members have successfully been able to attain political fame and have been able to represent Kenyans in the running of the government.
